Problem Dashboard Insights Design

Education’s Impact on Job Opportunities

Choosing a field of study is one of the most important decisions students make. This project explores how education shapes earnings and job quality across Ontario.

Explore Dashboard ↓

The Problem

Outcomes vary across fields

Graduates from different disciplines experience significantly different earnings and employment outcomes.

Lack of accessible insights

Students often rely on fragmented or anecdotal information when making decisions.

Data is hard to interpret

Existing datasets are complex and not designed for intuitive comparison.

Why It Matters

Choosing a field of study has long-term implications, yet the relationship between education, income, and job quality is often unclear to students.

  • Impacts long-term earnings potential
  • Affects job stability and satisfaction
  • Shapes career trajectories

What Can You Explore?

  • Which fields have the highest earnings?
  • How have outcomes changed between 2015 and 2020?
  • Do higher earnings correspond to better job quality?

Interactive Dashboard

This dashboard connects education to real-world outcomes by comparing earnings and employment quality across fields of study. It enables both high-level comparison and detailed exploration of individual disciplines.

How to use:
  • Select a field using the dropdown
  • Compare earnings trends on the left
  • Analyze employment outcomes on the right
Key Insight: Higher earnings do not always correspond to higher job satisfaction or field alignment, highlighting important trade-offs between income and job quality.

Geographic Distribution

This view provides additional context by showing how education and program distribution vary geographically across Ontario.

Key Insights

  • STEM and Health fields consistently show the highest earnings
  • Some fields have high employment but lower alignment with field of study
  • Earnings increased across most disciplines between 2015 and 2020
  • Job satisfaction varies independently of income

Design Approach

This project integrates multiple labour market indicators into coordinated views. A dumbbell plot highlights changes in earnings over time, while grouped bar charts enable comparison of employment quality metrics.

Interaction allows users to focus on a specific field while maintaining context, supporting both overview and detailed analysis.

Limitations

  • Aggregated data may hide within-field variation
  • Limited time range (2015–2020)
  • Focused on Ontario bachelor’s graduates
